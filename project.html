Carla Gilmore, a retired teacher from California, now helps run computer labs in Mexico and needs help buying three new laptops for her students.

Carla Gilmore was a multimedia teacher for 30 years at Cerritos High School in California. After retiring in Mexico, she started volunteering at two computer labs in Chuburna Puerto, a fishing village in the state of Yucatan. Overfishing has eliminated many jobs. The average daily income here is $20.

Computers and the Community:

The computer lab at the junior high school has 10 computers that serve more than 200 kids. On average, two to three kids share a computer. The community lab has 10 computers that serve 40 to 50 people per day. People rely on the community lab to complete critical tasks, such as registering their kids for high school and printing out important documents.

Problems from using old computers:

Ms. Gilmore expresses her frustration, “Having computer and internet access makes it easier for kids to complete their homework, but many do not have computers at home. The computers we’re using in the labs are old, between 4 to 12 years. There’s a constant need for replacement parts. Homework progress is lost because computers randomly freeze.”

These new laptops will help the labs replace old computers, significantly impact students’ ability to complete their homework, and provide access to the internet for the Chuburna Puerto community.
